Daily Post Style Guide

This guide is aimed at Editors and Authors on the Daily Post. This guide will give you an understanding of how the site works, as well as show you how to create compelling and beautiful layouts in your posts using some simple copy-and-paste-able HTML.

The Post Gutter

The gutter area is shown to the left of the content when reading the post. You can use this space for notes, quotes, asides, images, and more. You can also tell an image to go "full bleed" and stretch out of the content into the gutter — which really let's Cheri's amazing photographs shine.

An example of an image with the alignleft class applied to the blockquote.

Adding to the Gutter

You can add things to the gutter by adding the alignleft class to your element. There are aliases setup for align-left, leftalign, and left-align — they all do the same thing, but having multiple class names makes it easier to remember when editing.

For example, you can add the alignleft class to any image:

This will push the image into the gutter. The top of the image will align with the top of the next paragraph in your post. The width of the image will automatically adjust to fit the width of the gutter. The gutter is perfect for portrait and square images; Landscape images tend to feel too small.

Post Notes

Post notes are really useful for references and asides. Post notes will only be styled inside the gutter.

					
Note: This is my note.

Pull Quotes

You can put a pull quote into the gutter. Just use the pull quotes pattern and add the alignleft class to the containing element.

					
I felt a surge of power...

Full Bleed Images

You can create "full bleed" images, that is, images which stretch across both the Content Area and the Gutter. To let your image bleed across both columns, you can add the fullbleed or full-bleed class to a container element. Quotes and Notes

The Daily Post supports styling for a variety of blockquotes and notes.

An example of a plain blockquote.

Blockquote

Blockquotes, by default, are seperated from normal post content by an extra margin and a light background color.

					
This is my blockquote.

An example of a blockquote with the pullquote class applied.

Pullquotes

Pullquotes are a great way to show an excerpt from another post, a quote from an interview, or even as a way to highlight something from your post. Pullquotes will display their contents in a large, serif type and use a visual element to signify that they are quotations.

					
Four score and seven years ago...

Notice how the first paragraph (which uses the editorial-note class) is larger than the others.

Editorial Note

Useful when you (the Editor) is introducing a post written by someone else. Its also a great way to differentiate any text from the normal post content. Editorial Notes display text larger than normal paragraphs and in a darker color.

					
Today's post comes to us from Krista...
